SUBJECT:              2006 and 2007 Football Schedules – Non-District Opponents

DATE:                       December 2005

 

The NSAA is asking all schools who plan to play football during the 2006 and 2007 season, to submit a prioritized list of non-district schools they would like the NSAA to consider for their 2006 and 2007 football schedule.  The 2006 and 2007 NSAA Football Classifications and the 2006 and 2007 NSAA Football District Assignments to which you are assigned, are posted on your page and are also posted in the Football page on the NSAA web site (www.nsaahome.org).

 

Please rank in priority order the non-district schools that you would like the NSAA to consider for your football schedule.  The NSAA encourages your school to visit with all schools you are considering for your non-district games, so as to make sure that a school you’re considering is also interested in a possible game.  Also, please look at the adjacent Districts to your District for possible non-district schools, as the NSAA will do this to help fill all schedules. 

 

If you have already committed to playing an out-of-state school, please indicate the school and date for each game.  Also, for out-of-state games, please indicate the Home/Away for each year.  You do not need to indicate a date for all non-district games against Nebraska schools, since the NSAA will assign the week each game will be played.  The Home and Away will also be assigned by the NSAA for all games between Nebraska schools. 

 

The NSAA will not make any guarantees that your school will get to play the non-district schools that you list on the form.  The NSAA will make every attempt possible to accommodate your request for as many non-district games in your football schedule as possible.

 

Also, please be sure to indicate whether or not you share a stadium with another school or schools.  If you do share a stadium, please list the names of the schools who you share the stadium with, so as to assist the NSAA in the football scheduling process.

 

The completed two-year schedules (2006 and 2007) along with the two-year home and away designations for all Classes will be released on the NSAA internet web site (www.nsaahome.org) at 9:00 a.m. Central Time on February 2, 2006.  Each school is responsible for contracting officials for all of the NSAA assigned, home varsity football games.

 

The form needs to be completed by JANUARY 3, 2006.  Once you click “Submit” on your form, it will automatically be sent to the NSAA.

 

Thanks for your help!
